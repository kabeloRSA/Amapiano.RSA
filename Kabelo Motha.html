The Rise of Amapiano: How South Africa's Newest Music Genre is Taking the World by Storm

In recent years, South Africa has given birth to a new music genre that has taken the continent by storm. Amapiano, a fusion of deep house, gqom, and jazz, has become the soundtrack of the youth, dominating airwaves and dance floors alike. In this article, we'll delve into the history, evolution, and global impact of Amapiano, the music industry's newest sensation.


Amapiano emerged in the early 2010s in Pretoria, South Africa, primarily among the youth in townships. It was initially characterized by its slow tempo, heavy basslines, and soulful melodies. The genre drew inspiration from traditional South African music, such as Mbira and Maskanda, as well as international influences like deep house and electronic music. Over time, Amapiano has branched out into various sub-genres, incorporating elements from hip-hop, R&B, and Afrobeats.


Amapiano boasts a talented pool of artists, including pioneers like Sun-EL Musician, Moonchild Sanelly, and Kabza De Small. The genre has produced numerous hits, such as "Akanamali" by Sun-EL Musician, "Weh Mameh" by MFR Souls, and "Sandton" by Focalistic. These tracks have not only topped local charts but have also gained international recognition, with many Amapiano artists collaborating with global stars like Drake, Wizkid, and Diplo.


Amapiano's influence extends beyond South Africa's borders, with the genre gaining popularity in Africa, Europe, and the Americas. Its unique sound has captivated audiences worldwide, leading to sold-out shows and festivals. Amapiano has also inspired a new wave of producers and DJs, who are experimenting with the genre and pushing its boundaries. The global music industry has taken notice, with major labels and streaming platforms investing in Amapiano talent and content.


Amapiano's rapid rise is a testament to the creativity and innovation of South African youth. From its humble beginnings in Pretoria to its current global appeal, Amapiano has become a cultural phenomenon, uniting people across borders and generations. As the genre continues to evolve, it's clear that Amapiano is here to stay, leaving an indelible mark on the music industry and the world at large.

    Uncle waffles



    Early Life
    Lungelihle Zwane, born on March 30, 2000, is a South African DJ and record producer born in Swaziland. Zwane grew up in Eswatini, formerly known as Swaziland. Throughout her childhood years, she was raised by her grandma until she reconciled with her mother as a teenager, with whom she had a strained relationship. Zwane grew up listening to South African house and kwaito music from artists such as Black Coffee, Lebo Mathosa, and DJ Kent. Before her success as a DJ, Zwane used to be a television presenter on Studio 1 on Eswatini TV, but she was replaced in January 2022 after parting from the show. It was here that she first was introduced to DJ'ing by a friend who also worked at the studio, as there was a fifteen-year-old set of DJ decks there. She then emigrated to South Africa to attend university.

    Career
    Uncle Waffles learned how to DJ in 2020 during the COVID-19 lockdowns and claims to have practiced for eight hours each day. She signed with South African creative agency Kreative Kornerr, who managed to book gigs for her in the country's "heavily saturated" music industry. After filling in for a withdrawn DJ at the Zone 6 nightclub in Soweto in October 2021, she gained prominence after a video of her dancing to the song "Adiwele" by Young Stunna during her set went viral. Uncle Waffles soon ventured into music production and released her debut single "Tanzania" in March 2022 in anticipation of her debut extended play (EP), Red Dragon, which was released later that month. The single, which had reportedly taken her three months to perfect, was ranked by Rolling Stone as the eighth best afropop song of 2022, with critics describing her as "a global ambassador for amapiano." In April 2022, Uncle Waffles embarked on her first-ever tour of the United Kingdom and Ireland. The tour was closely interlinked with a series of shows organized by Piano People, which she headlined alongside DJ Maphorisa and Focalistic, and is deemed the first all-star amapiano tour in the region. Uncle Waffles released her debut music video for the track "Tanzania" in September 2022. In that same month, Uncle Waffles announced that she would be hosting a residency on BBC Radio 1. She was the only DJ that performed at the 2022 Global Citizen Festival in Accra. She partnered with KFC South Africa to launch an exclusive "Uncle Waffles burger" which was made available for purchase in local branches in December 2022 and later again in September 2023. She then embarked on a concert tour of the United States and then Australia. British publication hailed Uncle Waffles as one of the top breakthrough DJs of the year 2022 worldwide. Mixmag Streaming platform Audiomack also hailed her as an "#UpNow" artist in March 2023. Uncle Waffles' second EP, Asylum, was released in March 2023 and featured the track "Yahyuppiyah," which had been released as the first single from the project. The EP was certified gold by the Recording Industry of South Africa (RISA) within one week of its release. In April 2023, she became the first amapiano musician to ever perform at the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival. Her song "Tanzania" was sampled by American singer Beyoncé in a dance break on the Renaissance World Tour setlist, to which Uncle Waffles reacted with the statement "I'm crying, wow," on Instagram. She was nominated for Best International Act at the 2023 BET Awards. Uncle Waffles released the EP Solace (2023) as the follow-up to Asylum in what she called a double-sided project titled, "An Asylum of Solace." It was supported by the single "Echoes," featuring Tony Duardo, which was released on 4 August 2023.
